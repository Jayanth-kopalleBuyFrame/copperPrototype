Copper.co Discovery Framework

Salesforce Revenue Cloud & Analytics (RCA) - Migration from CPQ

1
Current State Assessment
Objective: Understand Copper's existing CPQ setup and identify gaps between current quoting capabilities and actual billing complexity for institutional crypto custody.
1
You're currently using Salesforce CPQ - walk me through what happens AFTER an order is signed. How does usage data (Assets Under Custody, trading volume) flow into invoices today?
2
How do you currently calculate custody fees when crypto values fluctuate daily? Manual exports? Scripts? Excel? Walk me through the actual process.
3
What's your month-end close process like? How many days does it take from usage data collection to final invoices sent? How many people are involved?
4
Where are the biggest manual interventions today in your billing process? (Pricing adjustments? Revenue recognition? Multi-currency conversions?)
5
How do you handle ASC 606 revenue recognition currently? Manual journal entries or partially automated? What systems are involved?
2
Business Model & Revenue Streams
Objective: Map Copper's revenue streams (custody, trading, prime services, subscriptions) to understand billing complexity and RCA value proposition.
1
Break down your revenue mix: What percentage comes from custody fees vs. trading fees vs. platform subscriptions vs. prime services?
2
For custody fees - how do you calculate the billable amount? Daily snapshots? Average monthly AUC? End-of-month balance? Does it vary by customer?
3
Do you offer volume discounts or tiered pricing? If yes, how do you track whether clients hit discount thresholds? (Real-time? Monthly? Quarterly?)
4
You've expanded to Hong Kong and Abu Dhabi - how many legal entities bill customers? Do you invoice in local currencies or standardize everything to USD?
5
What's your typical contract structure? Annual upfront payment? Monthly in arrears? Mix of both? How does this impact your cash flow vs. revenue recognition?
Complete Product Portfolio
6
Looking at your complete product portfolio (Custody, Staking, ClearLoop, Treasury Management, Copper Connect, Copper Network, Agency Lending, Securities) - what percentage of your customers use just ONE product vs. multiple products bundled together?
7
For clients using multiple products (e.g., Custody + Staking + ClearLoop), do you invoice separately for each product, or create bundled pricing? How does this complicate month-end billing?
8
Treasury Management and Copper Connect - are these add-on modules included with custody, or standalone products with separate pricing?
9
Agency Lending - when you lend client assets on their behalf, how do you split revenue with them? How is this calculated and reflected on invoices (gross vs. net)?
10
Copper Securities (your FSRA-regulated UAE entity) - does this have separate billing systems, different fee structures, or different revenue recognition requirements due to securities regulations?
3
Customer & Billing Structure
Objective: Understand who gets billed, how invoice structures work, and the complexity of institutional client relationships.
1
Who are your customers that receive invoices? Are they institutional investors (hedge funds, family offices) or the underlying asset owners (the funds' LPs)? Or both?
2
Walk us through a typical billing scenario: If a hedge fund has $100M in custody and trades across 5 exchanges in a month, how many invoices are generated and who receives them?
3
What is the relationship between an institutional client, their underlying assets, and your billing model? Do you bill per fund entity, per sub-fund, per asset class, or aggregated?
Volume & Scale
4
You work with 400+ institutions. Break this down: How many are actively trading vs. custody-only? How many have multi-year contracts vs. month-to-month?
5
What is your current monthly invoice volume, and what growth do you anticipate with your Hong Kong and Abu Dhabi expansion over the next 12-24 months?
4
Billable Events & Triggers
Objective: Identify what triggers billing, when charges are calculated, and how different products have different billing cycles.
1
What event triggers the creation of an invoice? Is it month-end close, a specific calendar date, trade settlement completion, or something else?
2
For custody fees - do you bill based on daily AUC snapshots, average monthly balance, or end-of-month valuation? How do you handle intra-month deposits/withdrawals?
3
For trading fees - do you bill per trade immediately, batch at month-end, or net-settle with the client? How do different exchanges affect this timing?
4
For staking rewards - when does the billing event occur? When rewards are earned, claimed, or distributed? If you take a percentage of staking rewards, how is this calculated and billed?
5
For prime services (lending) - how do you bill interest? Daily accrual? Monthly calculation? How does this integrate with other billing components?
6
Copper Connect (DeFi Gateway) - how do you bill for DeFi interactions? Per transaction? Monthly access fee? How do you track and price different types of DeFi activities (swaps, lending, yield farming)?
7
Copper Network (settlement services) - are you billing both parties in a settlement transaction? Is there a network fee vs. per-settlement fee? How does pricing differ from ClearLoop?
5
Pricing Complexity & Crypto Volatility
Objective: Understand how pricing models work, what variables drive final prices, and how crypto market volatility impacts billing.
1
Walk us through your most complex pricing scenario. What variables determine the final price? (AUC tiers, blockchain type, storage type - cold/warm/hot, volume discounts, contract term length?)
2
How do you handle crypto price volatility in billing? If Bitcoin drops 40% mid-month, does that affect custody fee calculations immediately, or do you use some kind of averaging mechanism?
3
Do you have different custody pricing for different blockchains? (e.g., Bitcoin vs. Ethereum vs. Solana) If yes, how is this reflected in invoices and product structures?
4
What percentage of your clients are on custom pricing vs. standard rate cards? For custom pricing, how do you manage exceptions and ensure billing accuracy?
6
Mid-Contract Changes & Amendments
Objective: Identify how contract amendments are handled, how pricing changes flow through to billing, and the manual effort involved.
1
Walk me through what happens when a client wants to add services mid-contract. (Example: They have custody-only and now want to add trading access) How long does this take from request to updated billing?
2
If a client significantly increases their AUC mid-month (e.g., deposits an additional $50M), how do you handle pro-rated billing? Is this automated or manual?
3
When you negotiate a price increase or volume discount tier change mid-contract, how long does it take Finance to calculate the billing and revenue recognition impact? What systems are involved?
4
How do you handle downgrades or service removals? (Client reduces AUC or stops trading) What's the process for adjusting ongoing billing and revenue recognition?
7
Data Integration & System Architecture
Objective: Map data sources, understand integration points, and identify where usage data lives and how it flows into billing.
1
Where does your Assets Under Custody (AUC) data live today? Is it in your custody platform, a separate database, or multiple sources? How is it aggregated?
2
How does trading volume data flow from exchanges (via ClearLoop) into your billing system? Real-time APIs? Daily batch exports? Manual reconciliation?
3
What's the latency between a trade executing and that data being available for billing? Hours? Days? Does this create any month-end close challenges?
4
What systems are currently involved in your quote-to-cash process? (Salesforce CPQ, ERP system, custom databases, Excel, other tools?) How do they connect?
5
If you had perfect integration, what would the ideal data flow look like? Custody/trading systems → Salesforce RCA → invoice generation → accounting system?
8
Pain Points & Breaking Points
Objective: Identify the specific scenarios causing the most pain, where current systems break, and what keeps Finance up at night.
1
What's the ONE billing scenario that consistently causes headaches for your Finance team? Walk me through a specific recent example.
2
What percentage of invoices require manual corrections or adjustments after generation? What are the most common types of errors?
3
Where do you see your current CPQ + manual billing process breaking as you scale? More clients? More products? More jurisdictions? More trading volume?
4
How much time does your Finance team spend on billing operations vs. strategic work (forecasting, analysis, investor reporting)? What percentage split?
5
If you could wave a magic wand and fix ONE thing about your quote-to-cash process tomorrow, what would it be and why?
9
Audit, Compliance & Reporting
Objective: Understand regulatory requirements, audit burden, and compliance needs that RCA's automated controls can address.
1
What audit and compliance requirements do you have? (SOC 2, ISO 27001, financial audits for investors, regulatory reporting?) How frequently?
2
How much time goes into audit preparation each cycle? What billing and revenue documentation do auditors specifically request?
3
Do you have regulatory requirements around billing transparency or fee disclosure for institutional clients? (MiFID II, SEC custody rules, etc.?)
4
How do you handle multi-jurisdictional tax compliance? (VAT in UK/EU, GST in Singapore/Hong Kong, sales tax in US?) Is this automated or manual?
5
What revenue reports do you provide to your board and investors? How long does it take to generate these? Are they pulled from multiple systems?
10
Success Criteria & Implementation Readiness
Objective: Define what success looks like, identify stakeholders, understand timeline constraints, and address concerns.
1
What would a successful RCA implementation look like for you in 6-12 months? Give me specific, measurable outcomes. (e.g., "Reduce month-end close from 5 days to 2 days")
2
Who are the key stakeholders we need to bring along on this journey? (RevOps, Finance, Sales Ops, Product, Engineering, Legal, Compliance?) Who has veto power?
3
What's your timeline for migrating from CPQ to RCA? Are there any forcing functions? (Audit deadlines, fundraising rounds, new product launches, regulatory requirements?)
4
What concerns do you have about migrating from CPQ to RCA? (Data migration complexity, business continuity during transition, learning curve for teams, integration challenges?)
5
Beyond billing automation - what other RCA capabilities matter most to you? (Revenue analytics and forecasting, renewal management, subscription lifecycle, dunning and collections?)
Key Context to Capture Throughout Discovery:
• Current team size (Finance, RevOps, Billing Ops)
• Technology stack and integration points
• Budget and ROI expectations
• Decision-making process and timeline
• Competitive evaluation (other vendors being considered?)
• Internal champions and potential blockers
