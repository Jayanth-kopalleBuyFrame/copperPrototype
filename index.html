Understanding Digital Asset Custody Operations

Stage 1: Product Economics

Goal: Understand how different products generate revenue and what drives pricing variability.

1
Walk me through your custody product. Is it just "store crypto, charge X%", or are there different rates based on storage type (cold/warm/hot), blockchain, or asset class? What makes one custody arrangement more expensive than another?
2
For staking - you're sharing rewards with clients. How does this work across different blockchains that have wildly different APYs? (Ethereum at 4% vs Solana at 7% vs Polkadot at 12%) Does your revenue share percentage stay constant or does it vary?
3
ClearLoop trading fees - do you charge the same rate regardless of which exchange the trade happens on? Or does Binance cost differently than Coinbase? What about trade types - spot vs derivatives vs OTC?
4
When a client uses multiple products - custody + trading + staking - do you bundle them at a discount? Or is each product priced independently? How do you handle clients who want custom packages?
Volume Dynamics
5
Let's say a client starts with $50M in custody at 0.5% annual. They grow to $150M. Does the rate change automatically? Do you apply the new rate to everything, or just the incremental $100M? Who tracks this?
6
For trading volume discounts - if a client crosses a threshold mid-month (say they hit $50M on day 20), do you recalculate all their prior trades at the new rate? Or does it only apply going forward?
Geographic Complexity
7
You have entities in UK, US, Hong Kong, and Abu Dhabi. If a UK client wants to be invoiced in GBP but their assets are custodied through your HK entity, how does that work? Does currency or jurisdiction affect pricing?
8
Your Securities entity in UAE - does it have completely different fee structures than your custody business? Can the same client use both, and if so, do you consolidate billing or issue separate invoices?

Stage 2: Operational Complexity

Goal: Understand the mechanics of how billable events are captured, calculated, and invoiced.

1
For custody fees based on Assets Under Custody - do you bill on end-of-month balance? Daily average? Something else? What happens if a client deposits $100M on day 1 but withdraws it all on day 28?
2
You support 600+ assets across 60+ blockchains. Does your system automatically aggregate a client's total AUC across all of them? Or does someone manually pull reports from each blockchain and sum them up?
3
Walk me through month-end. When do you freeze the data? How long does it take to go from "month ended" to "invoices sent"? What's the longest it's ever taken and why?
Event-Driven Billing
4
When a trade settles via ClearLoop - is that billable event captured immediately in your billing system? Or do you batch them up throughout the month? How do you ensure no trades slip through?
5
Staking rewards are earned daily, but different blockchains distribute them on different schedules. Do you bill when rewards are earned, or when they're claimed? How do you track this across 20+ chains?
6
For DeFi interactions through Copper Connect - does every transaction cost the same? Or does a Uniswap swap cost different than an Aave lending transaction? Who configured that pricing logic?
Data Flow Reality
7
Be honest - what percentage of your billing process is automated end-to-end vs involves someone exporting to Excel, doing calculations, and then manually creating invoices?
8
Where does the data live? Custody balances in one database, trading volume in another, staking rewards in a third? How do you bring it all together to generate one invoice per client?

Stage 3: Volatility & Variables

Goal: Understand how market volatility, asset fluctuations, and dynamic variables impact billing accuracy.

1
Bitcoin drops 40% in 10 days (we've seen it happen). A client has $100M worth on day 1, but it's $60M on day 10. How does this affect their custody fee? Do you recalculate daily? Lock in at deposit value? Use averages?
2
Let's say you bill custody fees in USD but the client deposited 1,000 BTC. Do you track the USD value daily? At month-end? Who determines the exchange rate to use - Coinbase price? Binance? An aggregate index?
3
Some assets are incredibly volatile (memecoins, low-cap tokens) while others are stable (USDC, USDT). Do you charge the same custody rate for a stablecoin as you do for a highly volatile altcoin? Why or why not?
Edge Cases
4
A client has assets spread across 15 different blockchains. Bitcoin on Bitcoin network, ETH on Ethereum, but also wrapped Bitcoin (wBTC) on Ethereum. How do you price this? Same rate? Different rates per network?
5
For staking - if Ethereum's APY drops from 5% to 3% mid-month, does your revenue share percentage change? Or does the client just earn less and you earn proportionally less?
6
Trading fees on a $10M Bitcoin trade - are you charging based on the USD value at time of trade? Or are there situations where you charge based on BTC quantity regardless of dollar value?
Invoice Presentation
7
When you invoice a client for custody fees, do you show them the calculation? "Your average daily balance was $X, rate was Y%, fee is $Z"? Or just show the final number? What do institutional clients expect to see?
8
If a client's rate changed mid-month (volume discount kicked in), how do you show that on the invoice? Two line items? Footnotes? Do clients ever dispute the calculations?

Stage 4: Change Management

Goal: Understand how contracts evolve, how mid-term changes are handled, and the operational burden of amendments.

1
A client signs up for custody-only in January. In March, they want to add trading. Walk me through what happens operationally. Who updates pricing? How is it reflected in billing? How long does this take?
2
You negotiate a rate change with a client - maybe they crossed $100M threshold and earned a lower rate. Does this apply to the full month retroactively? Or only from the day they crossed? How do you recalculate?
3
Real scenario: Client deposits $50M on day 1. Adds another $100M on day 15 (now $150M total, new pricing tier). How many different calculations go into that month's invoice? Who does them?
Contract Complexity
4
Do you have clients with special pricing arrangements? (First 3 months discounted, annual prepay discount, commit to $X AUC for Y months?) How do you track these exceptions and ensure billing is correct?
5
When a client wants to downgrade (reduce services or move assets out), is there a notice period? Minimum commitments? How do you handle the final prorated billing period?
Revenue Recognition
6
For accounting - when do you recognize custody revenue? When you send the invoice at month-end (service already delivered)? Or as the service is delivered throughout the month? Who determines this?
7
If you change a contract mid-year (say you gave a client a retroactive discount), how does Finance handle this? Do they adjust prior months' recognized revenue? Or just apply it going forward?
8
You mentioned agency lending where you share interest revenue with clients. How do you account for this - as principal (you earn 100%, pay out 75%) or as agent (you only recognize your 25%)? Does this vary by jurisdiction?

Stage 5: Scale & Growth

Goal: Understand growth constraints, pain points that intensify with scale, and what breaks first.

1
You have 400+ clients today. Where does your billing process start to crack? At 500 clients? 1,000? Is it the number of clients, or the complexity of what they're doing (more products, more assets, more jurisdictions)?
2
Your Hong Kong and Abu Dhabi expansion - does adding a new entity/jurisdiction mean someone has to manually configure new pricing rules, new currency conversions, new tax logic? Or is there a framework that makes this easier?
3
What's the manual work that you absolutely KNOW won't scale but you're doing it anyway because it's not broken enough yet? (Be honest - everyone has these)
Error & Exception Handling
4
What percentage of invoices get generated cleanly vs need manual review or correction? What are the most common errors? (Wrong rate applied? Missing usage data? Currency conversion issue? Volume discount not triggered?)
5
When an invoice error happens - how do you discover it? Client calls and complains? Finance catches it in QA? Audit finds it 6 months later? How often does each scenario happen?
6
Have you ever had a situation where a client's volume discount should have kicked in but didn't, and they got overbilled for months? How did you handle the correction - credit memo? Adjust future bills? Refund?
Team & Time
7
How many people touch the billing process from start to finish? (Operations pulling usage data, Finance calculating fees, RevOps reviewing, Controllers doing rev rec, etc.) How many hours per month-end in total?
8
If you 3x your customer base over the next 18 months, what happens? Do you need to 3x your Finance team? Or is there a way to scale without linear headcount growth?
Audit & Reporting
9
When auditors come in, what billing/revenue documentation do they ask for? How long does it take to compile? Is everything in one system or scattered across databases and spreadsheets?
10
Your board wants to see revenue by product, by geography, by customer cohort. How long does it take to generate these reports? Is it a button click or a 2-day Excel project?
Final Reflection:
If you could change ONE thing about how billing works today - not budget constraints, not politics, just pure wishlist - what would it be and why would it matter?
